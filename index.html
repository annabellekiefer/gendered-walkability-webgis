Gender-Sensitive Walkability Assessment in Salzburg

Urban walkability is often measured with general indicators such as:

  • 🚶🏻‍♀ Sidewalk width
  • 🚦 Street connectivity
  • 🏪 Presence of amenities

While these metrics are useful, they fail to capture safety concerns of pedestrian groups such as:

  • 🌃 Women at night avoiding poorly lit streets
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Caregivers limited by stairs or narrow sidewalks

Therefore, this project applies the NetAScore toolbox to build user-specific walkability indices in Salzburg, showing how different perspectives reshape what counts as walkable. The results allow urban planners and citizens to identify where inclusivity and safety can be improved.
